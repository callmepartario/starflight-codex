Historical Timeline
  • [2100]  Humanity discovers Endurium and begins to research its properties.
  • [2150]  Humanity discovers Endurium's application to superphotonic technology and begins exploring the cosmos. Mardan-4 is colonized and exploited for its mineral wealth. The Empire is born.
  • [2300]  Humanity encounters the Velox.
  • [2650]  Major Endurium finds usher in The Empire's Golden Era. Humanity's ships venture deeper into space than ever before.
  • [2675]  The Empire encounters the Spemin.
  • [2770]  The Empire encounters the Thrynn and Elowan.
  • [3000]  Start of The First Wave: Numlox and Phlegmak ships attack from coreward space.
  • [3120]  The Empire forms an defensive pact with the Velox to combat the Numlox and Phlegmak menace.
  • [3260]  First wave ends.
  • [3400]  Start of The Second Wave: Spemin, Gazurtoid, and Uhlek ships attack from coreward space and settle outward.
  • [3450]  The Noah Project is initiated.
  • [3454]  Launch of Noah 2 expedition, which colonizes Arth.
  • [3466]  Launch of Noah 6, a deep space expedition, in cooperation with the Elowan).
  • [3480]  The birthplace and heart of The Empire, Earth, falls when its sun mysteriously flares.
  • [3505]  Uhlek bombs hit the surface of Arth, creating a southern hot zone and forcing survivors underground.
  • [3520]  Arth's governments and societies collapse. The Dark Years begin.
  • [4400]  Arth forms a new governing council. An industrial revolution begins, the surface of the planet is reclaimed.
  • [4500]  Rediscovery of space flight (sublight).
  • [4594]  Rediscovery of Noah 2 colony and Endurium.
  • [4615]  Humanity's new superphotonic ships leave the Arth system for the first time.
  • [4620]  Present day.
Artifact Technology
  • Black Egg: Planetary destruction devices of Phlegmak origin.
  • Crystal Cone: Locates Control Nexus of the Crystal Planet.
  • Crystal Orb: Bypasses Crystal Planet defenses and allows ship to land on surface. Triggers conflict with Velox when stolen.
  • Crystal Pearl: Warps ship out of combat once ship systems sustain damage.
  • Dodecahedron: A transmitter that increases alien ships' ability to track you through hyperspace. Not recommending when moving through hostile aliens' territory.
  • Ellipsoid: Return to Velox (who refer to it as the "Focusing Stone" to establish friendly relations.
  • Flat Device: Projects a shield around Terrain Vehicle, defending it against attacks from hostile lifeforms. Purchased from Starport.
  • Hypercube: Hours of fun for the kids!
  • Red Cylinder: Displays coordinates of Ancient Ruins and Artifacts from the captain's Landing screen.
  • Ring Device: Assists in displaying continuum fluxes when in hyperspace.
  • Rod Device: Gives the ship's shields a chance to deflect laser attacks.
  • Shimmering Ball: A cloaking device that allows the ship to dodge incoming attacks. This effect can actually hamper a ship's ability to outrun a Uhlek warship's plasma bolts.
  • Tesseract: Halves the Endurium fuel consumed by the ship's engines during hyperspace travel.
  • Whining Orb: Translates Spemin language. Purchased from Starport.

Lifeform Value

Previously unencountered, larger, more intelligent and predatory lifeforms are more valuable. The further away from Arth a lifeform is captured, the more valuable it may be determined to be. The Terrain Vehicle is not equipped to capture floating or flying lifeforms.

Hostile lifeforms are easier to capture when stunned.

Dead lifeforms are worth very little.

Cargo Pods

Interstel Ships' cargo holds may hold up to 50 CM of material: Fuel, mineral elements, lifeforms, and Artifact Technology all contribute to the total cargo space used.

Each cargo pod installed to an Interstel Ship adds an additional 50 CM of space to the ship's cargo hold.

The ship's Terrain Vehicle also includes an additional 50 CM of storage space, which will be offloaded into the ship's cargo holds when returning to the ship.

Terrain Vehicle Mining

Minerals appear in higher concentrations on rocky planets in higher elevations. Difficult terrainand inclement weather will drain the Terrain Vehicles energy reserves faster.

Running out of energy means returning to your ship on foot, which can be dangerous. Replacing the ship's terrain vehicle is also expensive!

Profile: Interstel

Captains of the fleet, the situation has never been more grave. Within two years, the star our home planet of Arth's orbits will flare, killing off all life in the system. This phenomenon is not local to our star system: stars all over our region of space are flaring, coreward to outward. Your mission is to do anything it takes to find out why this is happening and put a stop to it. Failing that, find as many coloinzable worlds as you can. We'll need somewhere to begin again if Arth can't be saved.
